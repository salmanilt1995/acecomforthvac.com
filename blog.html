How to Claim Up to $10,000+ in HVAC Rebates in BC — A Complete 2026 Guide for Vancouver Homeowners

March 2026 • 12 min read • By AceComfort Team

If you are thinking about upgrading your heating or cooling system in 2026, you could be leaving thousands of dollars on the table by not taking advantage of the rebate programs available to British Columbia homeowners right now. Between FortisBC, BC Hydro, the Canada Greener Homes Grant, CleanBC, and municipal incentives, it is possible to stack multiple rebates and recover $10,000 or more on a qualifying heat pump, furnace, or boiler installation.

AceComfort handles the entire rebate application process for our customers at no extra charge. Here is a complete breakdown of every program available in 2026.

FortisBC Rebates — Up to $6,000+ on High-Efficiency Equipment

FortisBC offers some of the most generous HVAC rebates in Canada. As a FortisBC natural gas customer, you may qualify for the following rebates when you upgrade to high-efficiency heating equipment:

  • Heat Pump Installation: Up to $6,000 for qualifying air-source heat pumps that replace or supplement an existing natural gas furnace or boiler
  • High-Efficiency Furnace: Up to $800 for ENERGY STAR certified furnaces with 95%+ AFUE
  • High-Efficiency Boiler: Up to $800 for condensing boilers with 90%+ AFUE
  • Tankless Water Heater: Up to $800 for qualifying on-demand water heaters
  • Smart Thermostat: Up to $100 when installed with a qualifying heating system upgrade

Canada Greener Homes Grant — Federal Funding for Energy Upgrades

The federal Canada Greener Homes Grant provides up to $5,000 for eligible home energy upgrades, including heat pump installations and other high-efficiency HVAC systems. This program requires an EnerGuide home energy evaluation before and after the upgrade. AceComfort can guide you through the process and coordinate with certified energy advisors to ensure your application is complete and approved.

CleanBC Better Homes Program

The provincial CleanBC program offers additional incentives for switching from fossil fuels to electric heat pumps. When combined with the FortisBC and federal programs, the total available rebate can exceed $10,000 for a single heat pump installation — significantly reducing the upfront cost and making high-efficiency heating accessible to more Vancouver homeowners.

How to Stack Rebates — Maximize Every Dollar

The key to maximizing your rebate is understanding which programs can be combined. In most cases, FortisBC rebates, the Canada Greener Homes Grant, and CleanBC incentives can all be stacked on the same installation. The order of application matters, and the paperwork must be filed correctly to avoid delays or rejections.

This is where AceComfort makes a real difference. We handle every rebate application on your behalf — we know exactly which forms to file, in what order, and with which supporting documentation. Our customers consistently receive the maximum available rebate because we do this every single day.

Who Qualifies?

Most homeowners in Metro Vancouver who currently heat with natural gas and are upgrading to a high-efficiency system qualify for at least one rebate program. Renters and condo owners may also qualify depending on the program. The fastest way to find out what you are eligible for is to call us — we will assess your situation in minutes and give you a clear answer.

The Benefits of Heat Pumps for Vancouver Homeowners — Why BC Is Making the Switch in 2026

February 2026 • 11 min read • By AceComfort Team

Heat pumps have become the most talked-about home upgrade in Metro Vancouver — and for good reason. They heat your home in winter, cool it in summer, and can reduce your energy bills by up to 50% compared to traditional gas furnaces. Combined with up to $10,000+ in available government rebates, a heat pump is one of the smartest investments a Vancouver homeowner can make right now.

AceComfort is a certified installer for Mitsubishi, Daikin, Lennox, Bosch, and other leading heat pump brands. Here is everything you need to know about why heat pumps work so well in Vancouver and whether one is right for your home.

What Is a Heat Pump and How Does It Work?

A heat pump is an electric system that transfers heat rather than generating it by burning fuel. In winter, it extracts heat from the outdoor air and moves it inside your home. In summer, it reverses the process and works as an air conditioner. Modern cold-climate heat pumps operate efficiently even when outdoor temperatures drop well below freezing — making them ideal for Vancouver's mild coastal winters.

Unlike a furnace that burns natural gas to create heat, a heat pump uses electricity to move existing heat. This is dramatically more efficient — for every unit of electricity consumed, a heat pump delivers 3 to 4 units of heating energy. That is 300% to 400% efficiency, compared to a gas furnace at 95% at best.

The Top Benefits of Installing a Heat Pump in Vancouver

1. Heating and Cooling in One System

A heat pump replaces both your furnace and your air conditioner. One system, one maintenance schedule, one investment. In Vancouver, where summers are getting hotter every year — especially after the 2021 heat dome — having reliable air conditioning is no longer optional. A heat pump gives you both heating and cooling without installing two separate systems.

2. Lower Energy Bills

Because heat pumps are 3 to 4 times more efficient than gas furnaces, most Vancouver homeowners see a significant reduction in their monthly energy costs after switching. The exact savings depend on your home's size, insulation, and current system — but 30% to 50% reductions in heating costs are common. With natural gas prices rising in BC, the savings gap is widening every year.

3. Massive Government Rebates Available Right Now

BC homeowners can currently stack FortisBC, CleanBC, and federal Canada Greener Homes rebates to recover $10,000 or more on a qualifying heat pump installation. These programs are designed to accelerate the transition from fossil fuels to electric heating — and they will not last forever. AceComfort handles the full rebate application on every heat pump installation at no extra charge.

4. Reduced Carbon Footprint

BC's electricity grid is over 95% clean hydroelectric power. Switching from a natural gas furnace to an electric heat pump dramatically reduces your home's greenhouse gas emissions. For environmentally conscious Vancouver homeowners, this is one of the most impactful changes you can make — and the government rebates are specifically designed to reward you for making it.

5. Quiet, Comfortable, Consistent Heating

Heat pumps deliver steady, even heat without the temperature swings of a furnace cycling on and off. Modern inverter-driven heat pumps from Mitsubishi and Daikin are whisper-quiet — both the indoor and outdoor units. Many homeowners report that their home feels noticeably more comfortable after switching to a heat pump, with fewer hot and cold spots and better air circulation.

6. Increases Home Value

A modern heat pump system is a significant selling point in the Vancouver real estate market. Buyers are increasingly looking for energy-efficient homes with low operating costs. A new heat pump installation — especially one with documented rebate history and a maintenance plan — adds measurable value to your property.

Types of Heat Pumps Available for Vancouver Homes

  • Central Ducted Heat Pumps: Connect to your existing ductwork and replace your furnace and AC. Ideal for homes with existing forced-air systems.
  • Ductless Mini-Split Heat Pumps: Wall-mounted indoor units with no ductwork required. Perfect for older homes, additions, condos, and targeted room-by-room comfort.
  • Water Source Heat Pumps: Use a water loop for heating and cooling. Common in commercial buildings and multi-unit residential properties across Metro Vancouver.

Do Heat Pumps Work in Vancouver's Climate?

Absolutely. Vancouver has one of the mildest winter climates in Canada, with average winter temperatures between 2 and 7 degrees Celsius. Modern cold-climate heat pumps from Mitsubishi and Daikin are rated to operate efficiently down to -25 degrees Celsius and below — far colder than Vancouver ever gets. In fact, Vancouver is one of the best cities in North America for heat pump performance because the mild winters allow the system to operate at peak efficiency for the vast majority of the heating season.
