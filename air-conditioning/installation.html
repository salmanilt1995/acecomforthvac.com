New AC Installation

Cooling That's Designed for Your Home — Not Just Any Home

Proper AC installation starts with proper sizing. Too small, and your system runs constantly without reaching the set temperature. Too large, and it short-cycles — creating humidity problems, temperature swings, and premature wear. AceComfort performs a Manual J load calculation for every central AC installation to ensure your new system is the right size for your specific home.

We install Carrier, Lennox, Mitsubishi, Daikin, and other leading brands — selected based on what's genuinely best for your situation, not what has the highest margin. Ductless installations are typically completed in one day. Central AC installations involving duct connections typically take one day as well for straightforward replacements.

Central vs Ductless

Which AC System Is Right for Your Vancouver Home?

❄

Central Air Conditioning

Best for homes with existing forced-air ductwork. One outdoor unit connects to your furnace or air handler, cooling your entire home through the same vents as your heating system.

  • Ideal if you already have ductwork
  • Cools entire home uniformly
  • Typically lower upfront cost if ducts exist
  • Single thermostat controls whole home
🌀

Ductless Mini-Split AC

Best for homes without ductwork, condos, specific zones, or additions. An outdoor unit connects to one or more sleek indoor units mounted on the wall — no duct modifications needed.

  • No ductwork required
  • Zone-by-zone temperature control
  • Very quiet indoor operation
  • Can also provide heating

AC Installation Questions

Most ductless mini-split installations are completed in one day. Standard central AC replacements (where ductwork already exists) are also typically one-day jobs. We arrive with the equipment, complete the installation, test and commission the system, and walk you through operation before we leave — all in a single visit in most cases.
Proper sizing requires a heat gain calculation (Manual J for central systems) that accounts for your home's square footage, ceiling height, window area and orientation, insulation levels, and local climate. We perform this calculation before recommending equipment. Avoid any contractor who sizes your AC based on square footage alone — it's a shortcut that leads to the wrong system.
