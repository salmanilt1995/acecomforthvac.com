FAQs

Air Conditioning FAQs

Vancouver summers have become increasingly warm, with heat dome events pushing temperatures well above 35°C in recent years. Air conditioning is no longer just for comfort — during extreme heat, it's a health essential, particularly for children, elderly family members, and those with medical conditions. We've seen demand for AC installations surge across the Lower Mainland.
Central AC connects to your home's existing duct system and cools all rooms at once through the same vents as your heating system. A ductless mini-split has an outdoor unit and one or more indoor wall units, connected by refrigerant lines — no ducts required. Ductless systems offer more flexible zone control and are ideal for homes without ducts, additions, or when you only need to cool specific rooms.
We recommend an annual spring tune-up before cooling season begins. This includes cleaning coils, checking refrigerant levels, inspecting electrical components, and verifying thermostat operation. Regular maintenance extends the life of your system, keeps it running at peak efficiency, and catches small issues before they become expensive repairs.
