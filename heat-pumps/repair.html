Common Problems

Heat Pump Issues We Repair Every Day

♨

Not Heating or Cooling

A heat pump that runs but doesn't condition the air may have a refrigerant issue, a faulty reversing valve, or a failed compressor. We'll determine the exact cause and advise on the most cost-effective repair path.

🔊

Error Codes & Blinking Lights

Modern heat pumps communicate faults through error codes. We read and interpret manufacturer-specific codes to identify the exact component that needs attention — saving time and avoiding misdiagnosis.

❄

Ice Buildup on Outdoor Unit

Some frost on the outdoor unit in cold weather is normal — your heat pump has a defrost cycle for this. Heavy ice accumulation that doesn't clear, however, points to a defrost control issue, refrigerant problem, or airflow restriction that needs repair.

💧

Refrigerant Leaks

Refrigerant doesn't get used up — if levels are low, there's a leak somewhere in the system. We locate and repair the leak, then recharge to factory specifications. A top-up without fixing the leak is a temporary measure that will fail again.

📈

Poor Efficiency or High Bills

A heat pump running but consuming more energy than expected often has dirty coils, low refrigerant, or a failing component forcing the system to work harder. We restore efficiency with a thorough diagnostic and targeted repair.

🔥

Stuck in One Mode

A heat pump that only heats but won't cool (or vice versa) typically has a faulty reversing valve — the component that switches the system between modes. This is a common, repairable issue that our technicians handle regularly.

FAQs

Heat Pump Repair Questions

Error codes vary by brand and model. Common codes indicate issues with refrigerant levels, fan motor faults, sensor failures, communication errors between indoor and outdoor units, or protection circuit activations. Don't attempt to reset and ignore a recurring error code — it's the system telling you something specific is wrong. We read and diagnose manufacturer-specific codes as part of every service call.
Repair costs vary widely depending on the fault. Common repairs like capacitor replacement, sensor replacement, or minor refrigerant top-ups are relatively inexpensive. More significant repairs like compressor replacement are costly and may make replacement a better option depending on the system's age. We always provide a written estimate before any work is authorized — you'll know the full cost before we begin.
Most heat pumps carry a 5–10 year parts warranty, with some extended warranties covering 12 years on compressors for registered units. Mitsubishi and Daikin units installed by certified dealers often carry enhanced warranty terms. We check your warranty status before proceeding and help coordinate manufacturer warranty claims for eligible repairs. Labour is generally not covered by manufacturer warranties but is covered by our own workmanship guarantee.
