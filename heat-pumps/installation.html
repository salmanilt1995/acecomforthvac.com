Heat Pump Installation — Start Saving From Day One

A heat pump is the most financially compelling home comfort upgrade available to BC homeowners right now. It replaces both your heating system and air conditioner with a single highly efficient unit — and the combination of BC Hydro, CleanBC, and Canada Greener Homes rebates can offset a substantial portion of the installation cost.

AceComfort is a certified installer for Mitsubishi Electric and Daikin — two of the world's leading heat pump manufacturers, both known for exceptional cold-climate performance and long-term reliability. We install ductless mini-splits, central ducted heat pumps, and water source systems for strata and commercial buildings.

Every installation includes a proper sizing assessment, all required permits, professional installation to manufacturer specifications, system commissioning and testing, a walkthrough of your new system, and complete rebate application filing — so you receive every dollar you're entitled to.

🆗 Rebates Available on Heat Pump Installation:
  • BC Hydro: up to $6,000 (replacing baseboard heaters)
  • CleanBC Better Homes: up to $6,000
  • Canada Greener Homes: up to $6,500
  • FortisBC: up to $3,000 (certain applications)

FAQs

Heat Pump Installation Questions

Before rebates, a single-zone ductless system typically ranges from $3,500–$7,000 installed. Central heat pumps generally range from $8,000–$15,000. After combining available rebates (BC Hydro up to $6,000, CleanBC up to $6,000, Canada Greener Homes up to $6,500), many homeowners find the net cost is dramatically reduced — sometimes by more than 50%. We show you the full rebate picture before you commit.
Most single and dual-zone ductless installations are completed in one day. Multi-zone systems with 3+ indoor heads may take 1–2 days. Central heat pump replacements are typically one day for systems with existing ductwork. We arrive with all equipment and leave with your home fully set up, tested, and ready to run — including rebate paperwork submitted.
In most Vancouver-area homes, no — a properly sized cold-climate heat pump handles your heating needs year-round. Some homeowners choose a "dual-fuel" setup (heat pump + gas backup) for added redundancy or to qualify for certain rebate tiers, but it's rarely a practical necessity here. We'll advise you honestly on what makes sense for your home — not what generates more work for us.
Rebate programs generally have flexibility — pre-approvals are typically valid for a set window (often 12–18 months) and you can adjust equipment selection within program guidelines. We handle all communication with rebate programs on your behalf, so if anything changes, we update the applications accordingly. You're never left to navigate the paperwork alone.
